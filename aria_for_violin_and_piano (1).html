Aria, for Violin & Piano

A physically‑modelled bowed violin — Helmholtz string motion, Stradivari body resonance, sympathetic strings — set against a warm, layered piano. Synthesised entirely in the browser.

The Instruments

Studio Violin

The bowed string is synthesised, not sampled. A Helmholtz sawtooth‑family waveform is built from its Fourier series and shaped by a bow‑pressure term, then corrected so the 2nd harmonic sits near the ratio measured on real bowed strings. Two detuned unison voices add the slight inharmonicity of a real string; a soft nonlinearity models bow‑string coupling. An eight‑band chain — a low shelf, five resonance bells, a notch, and a high shelf — recreates a Stradivari‑class body: the A0/A1/B1 wood‑and‑air resonances, the 1.1 kHz notch between them, and the "bridge hill" around 2.8 kHz, with the G string voiced warmer and the E string voiced brighter. The full chain is broken out below, with the exact curve for each string. Open strings ring quietly underneath as sympathetic resonance, and phrases glide between notes the way a bow does across a fingerboard rather than re‑triggering.

Piano Synth

A small additive piano: four decaying partials per note, doubled and gently detuned for width, under a lowpass filter that closes over the note's life — bright at the attack, mellow in the tail. Chords are arpeggiated rather than struck, so the pedal wash builds the harmonic bed for the violin's line.

Room

A single convolution reverb, generated at load time from a short exponentially‑decaying noise impulse, stands in for the hall. Both instruments share one send so they occupy the same space.

The Piece

Opens with two bare oscillators — a hand‑cranked siren winding up through a whoop and settling onto D, the plainest sound the engine can make, before anything physically modelled enters. From there: D major, roughly 60 bpm. Eight bars of a rising‑then‑resolving progression are played twice — sparser the first time, fuller the second, with a soft harmony line under the melody on the reprise — before a final sustained chord and a fading high note close it out.

Reference points for the physical model

  1. 1973Schelleng, J.C. "The Bowed String and the Player." JASA 53(1):26–41 — measured harmonic ratio (H2/H1) used to calibrate the second‑harmonic correction.
  2. 2002Jansson, E.V. Acoustics for Violin and Guitar Makers, 4th ed. KTH Stockholm — body resonance frequencies (A0, A1, B1−, B1+) and bridge‑hill characteristics behind the EQ chain.
  3. 2004Woodhouse, J. "Bowed String Simulation Using a Thermal Friction Model." Acustica 90 — the Helmholtz‑motion Fourier series used for the source waveform.
  4. 2010Smith, J.O. Physical Audio Signal Processing. CCRMA, Stanford — general digital‑waveguide and bowed‑string synthesis background.
